10[underline]th[/underline],, month,
the place seemed quite familiar
to me tho' there have been several
alterations made since then.
Well! after tea Fanny and
Jane played some tunes on
the piano and we had some
dancing, indeed plenty of it,
to make up the number in
quadrilles the two shop girls
came in, and danced
a little. We spent a very
pleasant evening and did
not go to bed till a tolerably
late hour.. We four girls ie
Fanny, Jane, Lucy and I
slept in one room in which
there were two beds, it was
very pleasant to be all together.
